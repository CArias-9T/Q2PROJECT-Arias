Sisig

- Main Dish Recipe By user-583 -

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork belly, boiled and cut into small cubes

  • 1/2 lb pig’s ears, boiled and finely chopped

  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped

  • 3 cloves garlic, minced

  • 2-3 Thai bird's eye chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce

  • 1 tablespoon vinegar

  • 1 tablespoon mayonnaise

  • 1 tablespoon calamansi juice

  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

  • 1 egg (for topping)

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for frying)

  • Salt to taste

Procedure:

  1. Boil the pork belly and pig’s ears in water with a pinch of salt until tender, about 45 minutes. Drain and allow to cool. Once cooled, chop the pork and ears into small, bite-sized pieces.

  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Fry the chopped pork belly and pig’s ears until crispy and golden brown, about 8-10 minutes. Remove from the skillet and set aside.

  3. In the same skillet, sauté garlic, onions, and chilies until softened, about 2-3 minutes. Be careful not to burn the garlic.

  4. Add the fried pork back into the skillet with the garlic, onion, and chilies. Stir in the soy sauce, vinegar, mayonnaise, calamansi juice, and black pepper. Let it cook for 3-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Transfer the sisig to a sizzling plate and crack an egg on top. The egg will cook on the hot plate as you stir the sisig.
